Payment Ecosystem

Learn how customers, merchants, gateways, processors, networks and banks work together to move money securely.

What is a Payment Ecosystem?

A Payment Ecosystem is a collection of organizations, technologies and processes that enable electronic payments.

Every successful payment involves multiple participants communicating securely behind the scenes.

Why Does It Exist?

Security

Protect customer payment information.

Trust

Ensure money reaches the right destination.

Scalability

Handle millions of transactions daily.

Compliance

Meet banking and regulatory requirements.

Key Participants

Customer

Initiates the payment.

Merchant

Receives the payment.

Payment Gateway

Captures and secures payment data.

Processor

Routes payment requests.

Card Network

Visa, Mastercard, RuPay.

Issuer Bank

Customer's bank.

Acquirer Bank

Merchant's bank.

Transaction Lifecycle

  1. Customer initiates payment.
  2. Merchant receives request.
  3. Gateway captures data.
  4. Processor routes transaction.
  5. Network forwards request.
  6. Issuer validates account.
  7. Authorization response generated.
  8. Merchant receives approval.
  9. Clearing occurs.
  10. Settlement transfers funds.

Authorization vs Authentication

Authentication

Verifies customer identity.

Authorization

Verifies whether payment is allowed.

Clearing vs Settlement

Clearing

Exchange of transaction information.

Settlement

Actual transfer of money.

Real World Example

  • Merchant = Amazon
  • Network = Visa
  • Issuer = Your Bank
  • Acquirer = Amazon Bank
